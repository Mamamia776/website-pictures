Merit Badges

 
 
 
 
 

    Merit badges are awards a scout can earn that earned on “merit”. Meaning, a scout does the required work, based on the topic of the merit badge, then meets with a counsellor for review and, if approved, receives a badge for that activity.


    For example: Troop 659 did the First Aid merit badge together. In a single afternoon, a merit badge counsellor by the name of Dr. Stephen Smith came to St Marina and led the lesson on proper care and treatment of an injured person. Topics ranged from minor cuts to life-saving techniques. Afterwards, the attending scouts each got the merit badge.


    Merit Badges are required for scouts to advance from the rank of First Class to Star, Life and then finally Eagle. Merit badges are a fun way to learn new things, try new activities and gain both knowledge and confidence.


    Troop 659 will do at least two (2) merit badges a year as a troop; one Eagle required and one standard. But it is expected of each scout (and their parents) that a scout pursue merit badges on their own - either by themselves, with friends or their patrol.
